# Masterclass: Intraday and Scalping Strategies for Indian Indices (Nifty, BankNifty, Sensex)

## Introduction: The Philosophy of Scalping vs. Intraday
As a senior financial analyst, I define the distinction clearly[cite: 1]:
* **Scalping:** High-frequency trading, capturing small price moves (5-15 points on index).[cite: 1]
* **Scalping Requirements:** Requires intense focus, tight discipline, and low latency.[cite: 1]
* **Intraday Trading:** Aiming for larger moves (50-200+ points on index) within a single session.[cite: 1]
* **Intraday Focus:** Focuses on trend identification and momentum.[cite: 1]

---

## Strategy 1: VWAP Reversion (The Gold Standard for Intraday)
**Applicability:** Nifty, BankNifty[cite: 1]
**Timeframe:** 1-minute or 5-minute charts[cite: 1].

### The Concept
VWAP (Volume Weighted Average Price) is the institutional benchmark[cite: 1]. Price tends to revert to VWAP when overextended[cite: 1].

### The Setup
1. Plot the **VWAP** indicator[cite: 1].
2. Add **1st and 2nd Standard Deviation bands** (VWAP bands)[cite: 1].
3. **Long Entry:** Price touches/approaches the Lower Band 1 or 2, and shows a bullish reversal candle (e.g., Hammer, Bullish Engulfing) while volume spikes[cite: 1].
4. **Short Entry:** Price touches/approaches Upper Band 1 or 2, and shows a bearish reversal candle[cite: 1].
5. **Exit:** Target the mean (the VWAP line itself)[cite: 1].

## Strategy 2: ORB (Opening Range Breakout)
**Applicability:** BankNifty (High Volatility)[cite: 1]
**Timeframe:** 5-minute[cite: 1].

### The Concept
The first 15-30 minutes of the market define the day's sentiment as institutions take positions[cite: 1].

### The Setup
1. Mark the High and Low of the first 15 minutes of trading[cite: 1].
2. **Breakout:** If the price breaks the High with volume, go Long[cite: 1].
3. **Breakdown:** If the price breaks the Low with volume, go Short[cite: 1].
4. **Risk Management:** Place stop-loss at the mid-point of the range[cite: 1].

## Strategy 3: Scalping with EMA Crossovers (The "Sniper" Method)
**Applicability:** Nifty, Sensex (Lower slippage assets)[cite: 1]
**Timeframe:** 1-minute[cite: 1].

### The Concept
Using fast-moving averages to catch short-term momentum bursts[cite: 1].

### The Setup
1. Use **9 EMA** (Exponential Moving Average) and **21 EMA**[cite: 1].
2. **Long:** 9 EMA crosses above 21 EMA; ensure price is above both[cite: 1].
3. **Short:** 9 EMA crosses below 21 EMA; ensure price is below both[cite: 1].
4. **Execution:** This is a pure scalping strategy[cite: 1]. Enter immediately on the crossover and exit as soon as the candle color changes or the EMAs threaten to touch[cite: 1].

## Core Rules for Beginners
1. **The 2% Rule:** Never risk more than 2% of your capital on a single trade[cite: 1].
2. **Brokerage Consideration:** In scalping, transaction costs (brokerage + STT + exchange charges) can eat your profits; use a flat-fee broker[cite: 1].
3. **The "Stop Loss" Law:** On Nifty/BankNifty, if your SL is hit, exit immediately[cite: 1]. Never turn an intraday trade into a "positional" trade to avoid losses[cite: 1].
4. **Index Specifics:**
    * **BankNifty:** Extremely volatile; requires wider stop losses[cite: 1].
    * **Nifty:** More stable, better for trend following[cite: 1].
    * **Sensex:** High liquidity, good for scalping during expiry days[cite: 1].
